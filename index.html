About

A lifetime spent
decoding the mind

Dr. Parag Jasani is an independent researcher and the creator of the Dichotomised Operating System (DOS) Model — a psychological and evolutionary framework that reveals the precise mechanistic functions behind human thought, behaviour, and social conflict.

Unlike conventional neuroscience, which maps the brain in terms of signals and regions, Dr. Jasani's approach treats the mind as a system — one whose hidden operating logic can be traced back to the process of natural selection. The result is not abstract philosophy, but a practical, information-processing manual for the human mind.

His research spans over 15 years and addresses questions science has long left unanswered: why we dream, why we meditate, why we feel superior to others, why language exists, and what truly drives thought. Each answer is derived from first principles — the deep logic of evolution itself.

His book Mechanisms of the Mind lays out the complete DOS Model. The Heal The World initiative extends that framework toward global peace — built on the conviction that understanding the mind is the only lasting path to resolving human conflict.

The DOS Model

How the mind actually works

The Dichotomised Operating System Model is a causal, not correlational, framework. It asks why — and derives answers from the first principles of natural selection rather than opinion or observation alone.

01
Two Hemispheres, Two Systems

The left hemisphere uses direct logic while the right uses contextual logic. Together they form a dichotomised operating architecture — processing every interaction simultaneously to optimise for survival and reproduction.

02
Evolution as the Root Cause

All complex human behaviours — social conflicts, emotional states, biases — are driven by a singular underlying evolutionary goal. Natural selection is the hidden operating system running your daily mental life.

03
The Self as a Software Agent

The DOS Model treats the "self" as a specific mental mechanism — a software agent designed to process information in ways that optimise natural selection. Understanding this agent explains why we behave the way we do.

04
Logic Over Sentiment

By understanding the precise information-processing logic of the mind, we can replace reactive conflict with clear, causal understanding. Every bias and every social rift has a mechanistic explanation — and therefore a resolution.

05
A Causal Approach

Conventional brain science accumulates correlational data. The DOS Model asks why. It is a systems-thinking framework that reveals root causes of mental phenomena rather than merely cataloguing their effects.

06
Universal Application

From meditation and sleep to language, gender differences, and the superiority bias — the DOS Model provides coherent, first-principles explanations for the full spectrum of human mental experience. One framework. Every phenomenon.
